• 1 small gobhi
  • 2 medium potatoes
  • 1/2 cup shredded cabbage
  • 2 tbsp frozen/fresh peas
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 large tomato
  • 2 green chillies
  • 1 inch ginger
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • Oil for shallow frying
  • 2 tbsp oil for cooking
  • 2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp carom seeds
  • 1 tsp salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp hing or asafoetida
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp dry fenugreek leaves
  • Some coriander leaves (cilantro)
  • 1 tsp anardana powder
  • 2 tbsp cream
  • 1 tsp garam masala
Preparation:
  • Cut cauliflower into medium sized florets and wash them.
  • Peel the potatoes and cut them into wedges. Then wash them thoroughly.
  • Cut and blend the onion to make paste.
  • Prepare ginger-garlic paste.
  • Puree the tomato.
  • Slit green chillies into long thin pieces
  • Wash and chop cilantro
Steps:
  1. In a wok, shallow fry cauliflower florets in heated oil.
  2. Next, shallow fry potatoes till crisp.
  3. Shallow fry cabbage and put aside in a plate covered with tissue paper when done.
  4. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a wok and add cumin and carom seeds.
  5. Add onion paste and fry until golden brown. Then add ginger-garlic paste and green chiliies.
  6. Once the raw aroma goes away, add tomato puree and mix spices - hing, red chilli powder, salt, turmeric powder, coriander powder, anardana powder. Cover and cook until the mixture releases oil.
  7. Add green peas and cream. Cover and cook for 4-5 minutes on medium flame.
  8. Add garam masala and fenugreek leaves (crushing them by hand). Cook for another 2 minutes.
  9. Add cilantro and the fried veggies. Mix well. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  10. Ready to serve.
